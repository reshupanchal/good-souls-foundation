Our Flagship Initiatives

Creating sustainable impact through healthcare, education and livelihood development.

Project Aarogya

Healthcare & Medical Relief

Initiative:
The Medical Assistance Fund & Community Outreach

We intend to provide a lifeline to families facing medical emergencies. This ongoing project focuses on bridging the financial gap for life-saving surgeries and chronic disease management.

The Work:
We will vet applications from economically weaker sections (EWS) to provide direct financial aid for hospital bills, diagnostic tests, and essential medications.

Impact Goal
Provide medical relief to 50 individuals in our first year of operations.

Project Vidya

Advancing Education

Initiative:
The School-Ready Kit Distribution

Education will serve as our most powerful tool for systemic change, breaking down barriers like the prohibitive cost of basic uniforms or notebooks.

The Work:
We will identify high-achieving students from low-income households to equip them with physical study materials and essential stationery.

Scholarship Program:
Applications will be opened for our Merit-cum-Means Scholarship covering tuition and examination fees for students at risk of dropping out.

Success Metric
Enroll the first cohort of 20 scholarship students by the next academic cycle.

Project Swavalamban

Livelihood & Skill Development

Initiative:
Micro-Enterprise Incubation

We will look beyond immediate relief to actively build long-term, sustainable self-reliance.

The Work:
We will conduct training workshops covering digital literacy and basic entrepreneurship.

Seed Support:
Post-training, we provide small "Step-Up Grants" to help individuals purchase equipment and start their own micro-businesses.

Impact Metric
Empower 10+ women and youth to start home-based ventures.

Eligibility / Selection Process

A transparent and accountable process ensuring assistance reaches those most in need.

Core Eligibility

To be eligible for assistance under our Healthcare, Education, or Livelihood programs, applicants must generally meet the following criteria:

Economic Status

Preference is given to individuals holding a valid BPL ration card, EWS certificate, or those with an annual family income below ₹2.5 Lakhs.

Documentation

Applicants must provide Aadhaar Card and proof of residency.

Non-Discrimination

Selection is made regardless of caste, creed, religion, or gender.

Program-Specific Criteria

  • Priority for life-threatening conditions and emergency surgeries.
  • Medical prescriptions, hospital estimates and diagnostic reports required.
  • Income Certificate required.
  • Preference for applicants not adequately covered under insurance or Ayushman Bharat.

  • Academic record and commitment to education assessed.
  • Admission Letter / Fee Structure required.
  • Income Certificate required.
  • Bonafide Certificate required.
  • Preference for first-generation learners, orphaned children and single-parent households.

  • Basic skill readiness or willingness to undergo vocational training.
  • Assessment of business viability.
  • Priority for women-led households and unemployed youth.
